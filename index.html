Our Mission

We believe talent should never be limited by access.

ARC Foundation is dedicated to empowering ambitious students through three interlocking pillars: scholarship, mentorship, and leadership. Together they create a holistic ecosystem for growth.

01

Scholarship

We provide financial support that recognizes students who demonstrate initiative and creativity, ensuring financial barriers do not limit opportunity. Our Career Catalyst Scholarship rewards drive, not just GPA.

02

Mentorship

Through one-on-one mentorship, students are paired with experienced professionals, alumni, and industry leaders who provide career guidance, networking, and professional support tailored to their specific goals.

03

Leadership

ARC hosts workshops, panels, leadership programs, and ambassador initiatives that cultivate confidence, community engagement, and professional readiness, preparing students to lead and invest back into their communities.

Founded 2025

Born from a moment that demanded action.

In June 2025, nearly 1,000 Howard University students faced outstanding balances, registration holds, and the real risk of losing their educational footing. Not from lack of effort, but lack of access.

Daniel Adegboyega, Jada Rabun, and Joshua Ndegwa saw it happen and built something to stop it. They recognized a pattern bigger than one campus: ambitious students held back not by talent, but by financial support, mentorship, and professional networks they never had access to.

ARC Foundation is their answer. Established at Howard University to invest in students through scholarships, mentorship, and leadership development, ensuring ambition is never stopped by circumstance.

The name says it all: Accelerating Rising Careers. We're here for students already in motion. Here to help them go further, faster.

How We Work

Three pillars. One mission.

01
Scholarship

We invest in who you're becoming.

Financial barriers end here.

The Career Catalyst Scholarship awards $5,000 to students who demonstrate initiative, ambition, and a commitment to building their future. Not just GPA. Four scholars are selected per cycle.

Recipients receive financial support, a dedicated mentor, access to exclusive professional development, and full membership in the ARC community.

Apply for Scholarship →
02
Mentorship

Every student deserves a guide.

One-on-one, built around you.

ARC pairs each student with a professional mentor who has walked the path they're on. Someone who understands the specific challenges of building a career from a place of limited access.

Mentors provide career guidance, networking introductions, resume feedback, and ongoing accountability throughout the program year.

03
Leadership

Built to lead beyond the classroom.

Programming that prepares you.

ARC Fellows participate in workshops, speaker series, and professional development sessions designed to build the skills that classrooms rarely teach: negotiation, networking, and navigating corporate environments.

Leadership development is woven into every touchpoint of the ARC experience, from our first cohort session to graduation and beyond.

Co-Founder

Daniel Adegboyega

Junior, Economics and Computer Science Major from London, England

At Howard University, Daniel combines his studies in Economics and Computer Science with a passion for finance, entrepreneurship, and technology. During his freshman summer, Daniel worked as a Private Credit Summer Analyst in Direct Lending at Blackstone, where he focused on underwriting, credit analysis, and deal evaluation within private credit markets. He serves as an Analyst in the Howard University Investment Group and has developed experience in investment research, financial analysis, and strategic problem-solving.

During his sophomore summer, Daniel worked as a Strategic Capital Solutions Summer Analyst at New Catalyst Strategic Partners, where he focused on Deal Origination for emerging GP seeding and accelerating opportunities. His previous experiences include participation in programs such as AltFinance and other selective investment-focused training experiences.

Daniel believes that talent should never be limited by access and co-founded ARC Foundation to help students gain the mentorship, resources, and opportunities needed to achieve their full potential. He is passionate about building pathways that empower future leaders to create meaningful impact within their communities and beyond.

This upcoming summer, he will be heading to Goldman Sachs as a Global Banking & Markets Summer Analyst.

Co-Founder

Joshua Ndegwa

Junior, Finance Major from Nairobi, Kenya

At Howard University, Joshua is passionate about using finance to drive economic growth and expand opportunities in emerging markets. He serves as the Senior Vice President of Investments for the Howard University Investment Group, where he has gained extensive experience in portfolio management and financial analysis.

Joshua was recognized as Howard University's Student of the Year and was one of only two freshmen awarded the prestigious HPS Endowment Scholarship.

During his sophomore summer, Joshua worked as a Private Credit Asset Management Summer Analyst at HPS Investment Partners, gaining exposure to institutional credit investing and portfolio management. His long-term goal is to establish a private equity firm in Kenya that supports local businesses and strengthens the economy.

Joshua believes ARC's mission is critical because mentorship, financial support, and access can unlock opportunities for talented students who are ready to create lasting impact.

This upcoming summer, he will be heading to Morgan Stanley as an Investment Banking Summer Analyst.

Co-Founder

Jada Rabun

Junior, Finance Major from Los Angeles, California

At Howard University, Jada is committed to financial education, leadership, and community empowerment. She serves as Treasurer of the Collegiate 100 and has dedicated herself to helping students navigate both their academic and professional journeys.

During her sophomore summer, Jada participated in the Discover Us Banking & Finance Fellowship at U.S. Bank, where she gained exposure to the financial services industry and developed a deeper understanding of career pathways within banking.

During her freshman year, she competed in Goldman Sachs Market Madness 2025, where her team placed first, securing a $1 million prize for the university.

She aspires to build a career in finance while creating opportunities that help underserved communities build long-term economic stability. Jada connects deeply with ARC's mission because she understands how access to mentorship, guidance, and resources can transform a student's future. She is passionate about ensuring that ambitious students receive the support necessary to achieve their goals and become leaders in their communities.
